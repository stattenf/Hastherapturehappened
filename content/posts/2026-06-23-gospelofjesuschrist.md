In March 2026, River Wilde published a lengthy post at gospelofjesuschrist.blog arguing that the Rapture is imminent and must occur sometime in 2026. The logic is impressively architectural: Israel was reborn in 1948, a biblical generation is 70–80 years, Christ was crucified in 33 AD, and 2,000 years of human history therefore point to a Second Coming in 2033. Subtract seven years for the Tribulation, and the Rapture must happen before 2026 gets away from us.

Wilde also cites Donald Trump's December 2025 "Board of Peace" as evidence that the Daniel 9:27 peace covenant is nearly signed, and notes that Mark 13:32 forbids knowing the day and hour — but, he argues, not the year. The Rapture could therefore happen "any day now," which is a phrase that has served end-times bloggers well since approximately the first century.

As of June 23rd, 2026, we are more than halfway through the year Wilde identified. The Board of Peace has not yet triggered the Tribulation, and believers remain on Earth, many of them still paying their mortgages.
